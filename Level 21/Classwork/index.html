Eagles

Eagles are large, powerful birds of prey known for their keen eyesight, strong talons, and hunting skills. They belong to the family Accipitridae and are found worldwide, except in Antarctica. Eagles have broad wings, which allow them to soar at great heights. They primarily feed on fish, small mammals, and birds.

Some notable species include:

1.The Bald Eagle (Haliaeetus leucocephalus), which is the national bird of the United States. 2. The Golden Eagle (Aquila chrysaetos), known for its strength and hunting prowess.

Eagles typically build large nests, called eyries, in tall trees or on cliffs. They are also symbols of freedom, strength, and courage in many cultures.
